What Is Root & Fruit?

Root & Fruit is a community-built integrity framework for evaluating political candidates and policies through the lens of Black social justice. It was designed in response to a persistent gap in voter education tools: most scoring systems reward proximity to power, not actual community impact.

The framework asks a different question: By their fruits, shall you know them. Not their party, not their rhetoric — their record.

The Five Pillars
🌱
The Root — Core Values (25 pts)
Evaluates the foundational worldview: anti-carceral stance, commitment to reparations and repair, solidarity with community over party, holistic safety (housing, health, economic security), and fiscal integrity. These are binary — either the evidence supports it or it doesn't.
📣
The Branches — Advocacy (10 pts)
Grants credit for active advocacy and organizing even when institutional power wasn't available. A candidate who fought for wealth transfer, CBO funding, housing, criminal justice reform, and intergenerational assets — but couldn't get it passed — still earns these points.
🍎
The Fruit — Outcomes (15 pts)
The hardest section. Scores actual deliverables on a 0–3 scale across five areas. A score of 3 means something was fully enacted, funded, and measurable. For community leaders, fruit is assessed through documented organizing wins, policy shifts they influenced, and lasting institutional change — not just legislation.
☀️
The Light — Visibility (10 pts)
Reflects how well-documented the subject's record is — across all evidence types: voting history, organizing records, journalism, public statements, and civic leadership. A community organizer with a rich documented record scores equally to an elected official. Low scores only reflect genuinely sparse evidence.
☠️
Toxic Soil — Penalties (deducted)
Three hard penalties for documented betrayal: Gatekeeper (−3, blocking other Black leaders), Plantation (−4, taking money from harmful industries), and Betrayal (−8, vetoing or killing a bill they promised to support). These are only applied with clear evidence.

The Two Pathways

Elected Officials are assessed on their voting record, sponsored legislation, budget decisions, and institutional deliverables. Having power and failing to deliver is treated differently from advocating without power.

Community Leaders — organizers, activists, civic leaders, and non-electoral figures — are assessed on their documented advocacy record: campaigns led, coalitions built, policies influenced, and community impact achieved. The Auto-Analyze engine is explicitly instructed to weight civic records on equal footing with legislative records.

Disclaimer
Root & Fruit is a community framework, not a definitive judgment. Auto-analysis is powered by Claude (Anthropic) and draws on publicly available information up to its knowledge cutoff. AI-generated assessments may contain errors, omissions, or reflect the limits of available public documentation. All scores should be treated as a starting point for research — not a final verdict. Users are encouraged to review cited sources, apply their own judgment, and adjust scores manually where needed.
